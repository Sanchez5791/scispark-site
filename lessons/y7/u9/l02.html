Plate Tectonics
板块构造
Why this matters today.今天为什么要学这个。
⏰ Warm-up recall · 热身回想
  • Around the edge of the Pacific Ocean — Japan, Indonesia, Chile, the west coast of the USA, New Zealand — earthquakes and volcanoes happen far more often than anywhere else.
  • This ring is called the Pacific Ring of Fire.
  • Why there?
  • Because that is where giant pieces of Earth's surface meet.
  • People called geologists and volcanologists study these pieces to help keep millions of people safe.
  • 在太平洋边缘 — 日本、印尼、智利、美国西岸、新西兰 — 地震和火山 比其他地方频繁得多。这一圈叫太平洋火环。为什么在那里?
  • 因为那是 地球表面巨大碎块相遇的地方。叫地质学家和火山学家的人研究这些碎块, 帮助保护数百万人的安全。
Learn · 学
Plate Tectonics
板块构造
  • Earth's solid outer layer is not one piece.
  • It is cracked into large slabs called tectonic plates.
  • Each plate is made of the crust plus the uppermost, solid part of the mantle.
  • The plates fit together like a cracked shell and move very slowly.
  • 地球的固态外层不是一整块。它裂成大块, 叫板块。每块板块由地壳加上 地幔最上部的固态部分组成。这些板块像裂开的蛋壳一样拼在一起, 并极慢 地移动。
  • Deep in the mantle, hot rock slowly rises and cooler rock slowly sinks.
  • This circular movement is called a convection current.
  • The moving mantle drags the plates above it, so the plates move.
  • 在地幔深处, 热岩石慢慢上升, 较冷岩石慢慢下沉。这种循环运动叫对流。
  • 移动的地幔拖动上面的板块, 所以板块会动。
  • Where two plates meet is called a boundary.
  • Plates can move apart (diverging) or move towards each other (converging).
  • At boundaries we get earthquakes, volcanoes, and mountains building up.
  • 两块板块相遇的地方叫边界。板块可以分开 (diverging) 或彼此靠近 (converging)。在边界会有地震、火山, 和山脉隆起。
  • This real map from the USGS shows the world's tectonic plates and their boundaries.
  • The lines of earthquakes and volcanoes trace out exactly where the plate edges are.
  • 这是 USGS 的真实地图, 显示世界的板块和它们的边界。地震和火山的连线 正好画出板块边缘的位置。

⛔ Common mistake · 常见错误
✘ Wrong / 错：
  • "Tectonic plates float on liquid molten magma."
  • "板块漂浮在液态熔融岩浆上。"
✔ Right / 对：
  • Plates do NOT float on liquid.
  • The mantle below them is mostly SOLID rock.
  • Under huge pressure over millions of years it flows very slowly, like a glacier — but it is not a liquid.
  • Magma is only found near volcanoes, not under every plate.
  • Plates slide on solid mantle, they do not float.
  • 板块不是漂在液体上。下面的地幔大部分是固态岩石。在高压和 百万年时间下它极慢地流动, 像冰川 — 但它不是液体。岩浆只在 火山附近才有, 不是每块板块下面都有。板块在固态地幔上滑动, 不是漂浮。
✦ Exam vs Reality · 考试 vs 真实
In the exam / 考试里：
  • In the exam, write plates float on molten magma.
  • 考试时写：板块漂在熔化的岩浆上。
Real science / 真实科学：
  • The mantle is mostly solid rock;
  • over huge time and pressure it flows slowly.
  • Magma is local, not everywhere.
  • 真实：地幔大多是固态岩石；在长时间和高压下慢慢流。岩浆只在局部，不是到处都有。

板块构造
✨ Summary · 总结
• Earth's solid outer layer is cracked into tectonic plates (crust + uppermost mantle).
• Convection currents in the mantle make the plates move.
• Where plates meet (boundaries) we get earthquakes, volcanoes and mountains.
• The patterns of earthquakes and volcanoes are evidence for where the boundaries are.
• Plates slide on mostly-solid mantle — they do not float on liquid.
• 地球的固态外层裂成板块 (地壳 + 地幔最上部)。地幔中的对流让板块移动。
• 板块相遇处 (边界) 有地震、火山和山脉。地震和火山的分布是边界位置的 证据。板块在大部分为固态的地幔上滑动 — 不是漂在液体上。
