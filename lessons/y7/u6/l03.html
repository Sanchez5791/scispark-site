Learn · Define

Producers, Consumers, Decomposers

生产者、消费者、分解者
💬
A producer is the food-maker. It does not need to eat another organism to get food.
生产者就是“造饭的”。它不需要吃别的生物来得到食物。
🔗
Producer = a tiny solar-powered kitchen.
生产者像“太阳能小厨房”。有光，就能自己做食物。
🧠
Producer starts with P; Plant often starts the chain.
记：P = Producer；P 也像 Plant。多数食物链从植物开始。
🖼️

💬
A consumer is the eater. If it gets food by eating a plant, animal, or other organism, it is a consumer.
消费者就是“吃饭的”。只要靠吃植物、动物或其他生物得到食物，就是消费者。
🔗
Primary consumer = first diner; secondary consumer = diner eating the diner.
一级消费者像第一位吃饭的人；二级消费者像“吃掉吃饭者”的人。
😄
A consumer is not lazy; it just does not own a photosynthesis kitchen.
消费者不是懒，它只是没有“光合作用厨房”。
🖼️

💬
A decomposer is the clean-up crew. It breaks dead matter into simpler materials that can return to the soil.
分解者就是“清理队”。它把死物分解成简单物质，让物质回到土壤里。
😄
Without decomposers, forests would become a giant pile of leftovers.
没有分解者，森林会变成超大型剩饭堆。
🧠
Decomposer = Dead matter breaker.
记：Decomposer 处理 Dead matter。D 对 D。

One sentence version: producers make, consumers eat, decomposers break down.
一句话版：生产者造；消费者吃；分解者分解。
😄
Mushroom wearing a plant costume still cannot photosynthesise.
蘑菇就算穿上植物外套，也不会光合作用。

生产者像充电器，消费者像用电的手机，分解者像回收箱，把有用材料送回去。

⚖️ 考试 vs 真实 · Exam vs Reality
📝 考试 Exam
In the exam, draw a food chain as one straight line.
考试时，把食物链画成一条直线。
🌍 真实 Reality
In real life, it is a food web with many connections.
真实自然是一张很多连接的食物网。

Q08 L2 Explain 3 marks
A student says all animals in an ecosystem are consumers. (a) State what a consumer is. [1] (b) Explain why a producer is NOT a consumer. [2]
✅ 答案 Answer
(a) A consumer is an organism that gets energy by eating other organisms. (b) A producer makes its own food by photosynthesis, so it does not need to eat other organisms.
(a) 消费者是通过吃其他生物获得能量的生物。 (b) 生产者通过光合作用自己造食物，所以不需要吃其他生物。
可接受 Accept:
  • (a) eats other organisms
  • (a) gets food / energy by eating plants or animals
  • (b) producer makes its own food
  • (b) producer uses photosynthesis
  • (b) producer does not need to eat other organisms for food
  • (b) plants make food using light, so they are not consumers
