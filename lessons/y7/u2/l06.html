LEARN · 学

Neutralisation

Neutralisation
  • What is neutralisation?
  • Neutralisation is the reaction between an acid and an alkali.
  • The acid and alkali cancel each other out.
  • The products are a salt and water.
  • When exactly balanced, the mixture is neutral (pH 7).
  • 什么是中和?
  • 中和是酸和碱之间的反应。
  • 酸和碱互相抵消。
  • 产物是盐和水。
  • 刚好平衡时, 混合物是中性的 (pH 7)。
  • Watching the pH change.
  • Start with an acid (low pH).
  • Add alkali a bit at a time.
  • The pH rises.
  • At the neutral point the pH is 7.
  • Keep adding alkali and the pH goes above 7 (now alkaline).
  • A pH probe or an indicator shows the change.
  • 看 pH 怎么变。
  • 从酸开始 (pH 低)。
  • 一点一点加碱。
  • pH 上升。
  • 到中和点时 pH 是 7。
  • 继续加碱, pH 就升到 7 以上 (变成碱性)。
  • pH 探头或指示剂能显示这个变化。
🟥 红牌 · 常见错误 / Red Card
✘ WRONG 错
Mixing an acid and an alkali makes an explosion.
把酸和碱混在一起会爆炸。
✔ RIGHT 对
Neutralisation does not explode. It makes a salt and water, and often warms up a little.
中和不会爆炸。它产生盐和水, 而且常常会微微变热。
⚖️ 考试 vs 真实 / Exam vs Reality
  • Exam vs Real Science.
  • In the exam, write reactions with a one-way arrow.
  • In real life, most reactions can go both ways and settle at a balance point rather than stopping completely.
  • 考试 vs 真实。
  • 考试时反应用单向箭头写。
  • 真实多数反应可以双向进行, 停在一个平衡点, 而不是完全停下。
🔗 比喻 / Analogy
  • Neutralisation is like mixing very hot and very cold water: the final result moves toward the middle.
  • 中和像把很热和很冷的水混合：结果往中间走。
