Why does a designer pick metal for one part and plastic for another? · 设计师为什么这个零件用金属、那个零件用塑料?
  • Why does a designer pick metal for one part and plastic for another?
  • An engineer designing a bicycle, a kettle or an aeroplane has to choose the right material for every part.
  • Copper for the wires because it carries electricity.
  • A light metal for the frame so it is strong but not heavy.
  • The choice depends on the physical properties of metals.
  • 👀 Look around you: pick up any coin. It is shiny, it is cold to touch (it carries heat away from your hand), and it does not snap when you press it. Those are metal properties.
  • 设计师为什么这个零件用金属、那个零件用塑料?
  • 工程师在设计自行车、水壶或飞机时,每个零件都要选对材料。
  • 电线 用铜,因为铜导电。
  • 车架用轻金属,这样又结实又不重。
  • 怎么选,取决 于金属的物理性质。
  • 👀 看看你身边:拿起任何一枚硬币。它有光泽,摸上去是凉的(它把 你手上的热带走),你压它也不会断。这些都是金属的性质。

The physical properties of most metals · 大多数金属的物理性质
  • Most metals: conduct electricity;
  • conduct heat;
  • are shiny (lustrous);
  • are malleable (can be hammered into shape);
  • are ductile (can be drawn into wires);
  • have high melting and boiling points;
  • are hard;
  • are dense;
  • are sonorous (ring when struck).
  • 大多数金属:导电;导热;有光泽;可锻(能被锤打成形);有延展性 (能拉成细丝);熔点和沸点高;硬;密度大;响(敲击时会发出声音)。

Two ways to test a metal · 测金属的两种方法
  • Electrical conduction: place the material in an electric circuit;
  • if the lamp lights, it conducts electricity.
  • Heat conduction: attach wax (or a pin held by wax) along a rod, heat one end;
  • the better the conductor, the faster the wax melts.
  • 测导电:把材料接进电路;如果灯亮了,它就导电。
  • 测导热:沿金属棒粘上蜡(或用蜡粘住针),加热一端;导热越好, 蜡熔化得越快。

🟥 红牌 · 常见错误 / Red Card
⚠️ Metal properties describe MOST metals, not ALL. "All metals are solid at room temperature" is WRONG - mercury is a liquid metal. "All metals are hard" is WRONG - sodium is soft enough to cut. Say "most metals", not "all".
⚠️ 金属的性质说的是"大多数"金属,不是"全部"。"所有金属常温 都是固体"是错的 — 汞是液态金属。"所有金属都硬"是错的 — 钠软 到能用刀切。要说"大多数金属",不要说"所有"。
⚖️ 考试 vs 真实 / Exam vs Reality
  • EXAM vs REAL SCIENCE: In the exam, "metal properties" is a fixed checklist to memorise (conducts, malleable, shiny, high melting point...).
  • In real science, these are TRENDS for most metals, with real exceptions: mercury is a liquid at room temperature, sodium is soft, and a freshly cut metal can dull as it oxidises.
  • Properties sit on a spectrum, not an on/off switch.
  • 考试 vs 真科学: 考试里,"金属性质"是一张要背的固定清单(导电、可锻、有光泽、 熔点高……)。
  • 真科学里,这些是大多数金属的"趋势",有真实例外: 汞常温是液体、钠很软、刚切开的金属会因氧化而变暗。
  • 性质是连续 光谱,不是开关。
🔗 比喻 / Analogy
  • Metal atoms are like a crowd that shares moving electrons, so electricity can pass through.
  • 金属像一群人共享会跑的电子，所以电能通过。
