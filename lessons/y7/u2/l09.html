⏰ 热身 / Warm-up
  • Pick one common object near you and ask: which property is useful here?
  • 看身边一个普通物品，问自己：这里真正有用的是哪个性质？
Why does one device need both metals and non-metals? · 为什么一个装置同时需要金属和非金属？
  • A phone needs metals to carry electricity (copper wires inside) and non-metals to stop electricity from leaking out (plastic casing).
  • The job decides the material.
  • 👔 Materials engineers choose metals and non-metals for each part of a product so it works safely.
  • 👀 Look at any coin in your pocket and any plastic bottle near you — one conducts electricity, one does not.
  • 一部手机需要金属来导电（里面的铜线），也需要非金属来阻止电流漏出（塑料外壳）。
  • 用途决定材料。
  • 👔 材料工程师为产品的每个部件选择金属或非金属，让它安全运作。
  • 👀 看看口袋里的硬币和身边的塑料瓶——一个导电，一个不导电。
😄 Non-metals are usually the “no thanks” materials: no shine, no bending, no easy electricity. 非金属通常像“三不”材料：不亮、不好弯、不容易导电。

Typical properties of metals · 金属的典型性质
  • Metals usually conduct electricity, conduct heat, are shiny, are strong, are malleable, are ductile, are sonorous, and have high density and high melting points.
  • 金属通常导电、导热、有光泽、强韧、可锤打、可拉成丝、敲击有声，密度高、熔点高。

Typical properties of non-metals · 非金属的典型性质
  • Non-metals usually do not conduct electricity, are poor heat conductors, are dull, are brittle, and have low density and low melting points.
  • 非金属通常不导电、导热差、暗淡、易碎，密度低、熔点低。

🟥 红牌 · 常见错误 / Red Card
✘ WRONG 错
If it is shiny, it must be a metal.
“只要有光泽就一定是金属。”
✔ RIGHT 对
Shine alone does not prove a metal. Some non-metals can look shiny too. Check conductivity and the other properties together, not one alone.
单靠光泽不能判断是金属。有些非金属也可以看起来有光泽。要把导电性和其他性质一起看，不能只看一项。
⚖️ 考试 vs 真实 / Exam vs Reality
  • In the exam, split elements into metals and non-metals.
  • In real life, there's a middle group called 'metalloids' — they act a bit like both.
  • Silicon is the most famous one (it makes computer chips).
  • “考试时分：金属 / 非金属。
  • 真实中间还有 'metalloid'（类金属），像 silicon（硅）。
  • 电脑芯片就用它。
  • ”
Where they sit on the Periodic Table · 它们在周期表上的位置
  • Metals are mostly on the left, non-metals on the right.
  • A student can read off whether an element is a metal or a non-metal from its position.
  • 📐 The reactivity series of metals is taught later in Y8 Unit 3.
  • 金属大多在左边，非金属在右边。
  • 学生可以从元素的位置判断它是金属还是非金属。
