Hook · Real-world spark
The Hammer and the Feather
锤子和羽毛
DouDou · The Hammer and the Feather
Hook video · 60-90 sec · Coming soon
Land together
同时落地

In 1971 astronaut David Scott dropped a hammer and a feather on the Moon — they hit the ground at the same time. The Moon is almost a vacuum: no air particles to slow the feather. 1971 年太空人 David Scott 在月球同时松手放下锤子和羽毛——两个同时落地。月球几乎是真空:没有空气粒子拖住羽毛。

Learn · Mechanism
What a vacuum is
什么是真空
No particles
没有粒子

A vacuum is a space that contains no particles. No air, no matter of any kind inside it. 真空 = 一个空间里完全没有粒子。里面没有空气,也没有任何物质。

Why they land together
为什么同时落地

The Moon is almost a vacuum, so there are no air particles to slow the feather down. With no air to hold it back, the feather falls just like the hammer. 月球几乎是真空,没有空气粒子拖住羽毛。没有空气拖着,羽毛就和锤子一样落下。

🟡 Note · 注意

Why objects fall, and how fast → a later Year 7 Forces lesson. 物体为什么会落、落多快 → Year 7 后面的力那一课。

🟥 Red Card · Common Mistake · 误区红牌

✗ Wrong: A vacuum is full of air. 真空里装满空气。

✓ Right: A vacuum has no particles at all — air is made of particles, so a vacuum has no air. 真空里完全没有粒子——空气也是粒子,所以真空里没有空气。

✦ Exam vs Reality · 考试 vs 真实

In the exam: A vacuum contains no particles. 考试写:真空没有粒子。

Real science: Even outer space has about 1 particle per cm3; a perfect vacuum cannot really be made. 真实外太空每 cm3 也大约有 1 个粒子;完美真空很难真正做到。

Wrap · Connect & summarise

课程完成

What you learned today 今天学到了什么

A vacuum is a space with no particles. 真空 = 没有粒子的空间。
No air particles → nothing to slow falling objects (Moon hammer + feather) and nothing to carry sound. 没空气粒子→没东西拖住下落物体(月球锤子+羽毛),也没东西传声。
A vacuum keeps a drink warm because heat cannot easily pass through it. 真空能保温,因为热不容易穿过它。
💡 Aside · 补充

Aerospace engineers design spacecraft that work in the vacuum of space; astronauts live and work in that vacuum. 航天工程师设计能在太空真空里工作的飞行器;太空人就在那种真空里生活和工作。
