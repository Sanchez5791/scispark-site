Learn · Mechanism
What is Matter?
什么是物质?
Matter 物质 has TWO properties
Has Mass 有质量
The amount of substance in an object
Measured by a weighing scale
Unit: grams (g) or kg
✓ Rock, coins, an apple, air
Occupies Space 占有空间
Takes up a volume (3D space)
Even invisible things like air take up space
Measured in cm³ or mL
✓ Balloon, beaker of water, air in tyre
L01_img1
DALL-E illustration: a smiling teacher pointing at a balloon and a rock side by side, showing both have mass and take up space (flat illustration style, warm orange palette).
Definition 定义
Matter 物质 is any substance that has mass 质量 and occupies space 占有空间(有体积).
Summary 总结
All matter has MASS and OCCUPIES SPACE.
Which everyday things are matter? 哪些是物质?
Rock ✓
has mass + takes space
Balloon ✓
air inside has mass + volume
Water ✓
liquid matter with volume
Light ✗
no mass — NOT matter
⚠ Common Mistake 常见错误
Air 空气 IS matter — it has mass and takes up space (you can feel it when the wind blows). Light 光 and heat 热 are NOT matter — they have no mass.
Material vs Matter 材料 vs 物质
Not all matter is called a material.
Definition 定义
A material 材料 is matter that is identified by a specific quality or use.

Every material is matter — but not every matter is called a material.
Matter 物质
+
Specific quality / use
特定质量或用途
=
Material 材料

Example: Water is matter. Water used in cooking (a specific use) = a material. 例子:水是物质。用于烹饪的水 = 材料。

Q1
Identifying Matter · 判断物质 MCQ
1 mark

Alex blows air into a balloon. He says: "The air inside is matter because it takes up space."
Is Alex correct? Alex 对吗?

A
Yes, because air occupies space and also has mass.
B
Yes, but only because air occupies space — mass is not needed.
C
No, because air has no mass so it is not matter.
D
No, because matter must be solid or liquid.
💡 Hint 提示
Matter needs BOTH properties — not just one. Does air have mass? Try squeezing a sealed plastic bag of air — you can feel resistance. Air has mass. 物质需要两个条件——不只是其中一个。空气有质量吗?
✓ Answer
A. Alex is partially right — air IS matter, but the correct reason is that air has BOTH mass AND occupies space. You need both properties, not just one.
Source: SciSpark practice question (Cambridge-style)
Q2
Matter in All States · 物质的三种状态 MCQ
1 mark
L2+

A student holds a sealed balloon filled with air. She feels the balloon pushing back against her hands.
Which statement correctly explains this? 以下哪项正确解释了这一现象?

A
Air has no mass, so the balloon floats and pushes back.
B
Air is matter — it has mass and occupies space, so it pushes against the balloon walls.
C
Air is not matter, but it still has volume, which causes pressure.
D
Only solids and liquids are matter — gases do not count.
💡 Hint 提示
Recall: matter must have BOTH mass AND take up space. Does air qualify? 回忆:物质必须同时具有质量和占据空间。空气符合吗?
✓ Answer
B. Air IS matter — it has mass (measurable on a scale) and occupies space (it fills the balloon and pushes outward). Gases are matter just like solids and liquids.
Source: SciSpark practice question (Cambridge-style)

Matter identification · Cambridge-style MCQ
1 mark

Which of the following is not a property of matter? 以下哪项不是物质的特性?

A
It has mass.
B
It occupies space.
C
It gives off light.
D
It can be a solid, liquid or gas.
✓ Answer
C. Giving off light is NOT a property of matter. Light is a form of energy, not matter — it has no mass and does not occupy space in the way matter does.

What you learned today 今天学到了什么

Matter 物质 = any substance with mass AND that occupies space. Both properties — not just one.
Air, liquids, and solids are all matter. Light and heat are NOT matter.
Material 材料 = matter with a specific quality or use. Every material is matter, but not every matter is a material.
Materials are matter with a specific quality or use — water for drinking, wood for building, air for inflating. Coming in Unit 2: how materials are classified. 材料是有特定用途的物质。Unit 2 讲分类。
