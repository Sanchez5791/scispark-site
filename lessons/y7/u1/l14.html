The water you drink, the ice, the kitchen salt, the air you breathe, the vacuum flask — all of it is this unit. From "what is matter?" to "a space with no particles". Today, connect it all. 你喝的水、冰、厨房的盐、呼吸的空气、保温杯——全是这单元。从「什么是物质」到「没有粒子的空间」。今天全串起来。

Learn · Mechanism
One connected picture
一张连起来的图
1
Matter → sizes of matter → particle model → atoms & elements → periodic table → element/compound/mixture → separating mixtures → particle arrangement → particle motion → particle attraction → changes of state → heating/cooling curves → vacuum. 物质 → 物质大小 → 粒子模型 → 原子和元素 → 周期表 → 元素/化合物/混合物 → 分离混合物 → 粒子排列 → 粒子运动 → 粒子吸引力 → 状态变化 → 加热/冷却曲线 → 真空。

Everything branches from one core idea — the particle model. 一切都从一个核心概念分支出来——粒子模型。

🟥 Red Card · Common Mistake · 误区红牌

✗ Wrong: The space between particles is filled with air. 粒子之间填满了空气。

✓ Right: The space between particles is empty — a vacuum. Air is itself made of particles, so it cannot sit in the gaps between other particles. 粒子之间是空的——是真空。空气本身也是粒子做的,不能塞在别的粒子之间的空隙里。

6 marks

Describe how the particles in a solid differ from the particles in a gas, in terms of arrangement, motion and attraction. 从排列、运动、吸引三方面，描述固体里的粒子和气体里的粒子有 什么不同。

🗣️ "Describe" = say what it is like. Give the facts in order. Describe = 说出它是什么样的，按顺序给出事实。
Saved ✓ 已保存
💡 Hint 提示
Compare three things one at a time: how they are arranged, how they move, how strongly they pull on each other. 一次比一项：怎么排、怎么动、互相拉得多紧。

In a solid, particles are arranged in a regular fixed pattern, only vibrate in place, and have strong attraction. In a gas, particles are spread out / random, move quickly in all directions, and have very weak attraction. 固体里粒子规则排列、固定，只在原地振动，吸引力强。 气体里粒子分散/随机，朝各方向快速运动，吸引力很弱。

Q03
Explain · apply L1
1 mark

A block of ice is heated until it melts into water and then boils into steam. Explain, using the particle model, what happens to the particles at each step. 一块冰被加热，先融成水，再沸腾成水蒸气。用粒子模型解释每一步 粒子发生了什么。

🗣️ "Explain" = give reasons. Say WHY, step by step. Explain = 给原因，一步一步讲为什么。
Saved ✓ 已保存
💡 Hint 提示
At each step ask: what does heat do to the particles' energy, and what happens to their arrangement? 每一步问：热让粒子的能量怎么变？排列怎么变？
Melting = pattern breaks so particles slide. Boiling = attraction is beaten so particles escape. 融化=排列被打破，粒子能滑动。沸腾=吸引被打败，粒子逃出来。
✓ Marking Scheme
Heating gives the particles more energy. In the ice, particles vibrate faster until the regular pattern breaks and they can move past each other — it melts to water. More heating makes them move faster still and the attraction between them is overcome, so they escape and spread out — it boils to steam. 加热给粒子更多能量。冰里粒子振动越来越快，直到规则 排列被打破，能互相滑过——融成水。再加热粒子动得更快，克服彼此的吸引力， 逃出来分散开——沸腾成水蒸气。

✓ Accept: heating = more energy [1]; ice->water: pattern breaks / particles slide past each other [1]; water->steam: attraction overcome / particles escape + spread out [1]. [3]
✗ Reject: "particles melt"; "particles turn into water" (the particles stay the same — only their arrangement/energy changes).
