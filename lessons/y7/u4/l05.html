Looking at Plant Cells观察植物细胞

Robert Hooke, 1665. He cut a thin slice of cork and looked at it under an early microscope. He saw tiny empty boxes and called them "cells" — like the small rooms monks lived in. That is how the word "cell" was born. Today a microscopist still does the same job: take a real sample, prepare a slide, and look at the hidden world inside.1665 年, Robert Hooke 切了一片很薄的软木, 放在早期显微镜下看。 他看到一格一格的小空盒, 叫它们 "cell" (细胞) — 像修士住的小房间。 "细胞" 这个名字就是这样来的。今天的显微镜技术员还是做同样的事: 取真实样本, 做切片, 看里面藏着的世界。

🟥 RED CARD · Common mistake🟥 红牌 · 常见错误
✘ The error✘ 错误想法
Every plant cell looks exactly like the textbook picture.每个植物细胞都长得跟课本那张图一模一样。
✓ Correct idea✓ 正确想法
The textbook plant cell is a general template. Real plant cells differ by job — an onion epidermis cell has no green chloroplasts, while a leaf cell is packed with them.课本那张植物细胞图是拼出来的通用模板。真实植物细胞按工作 不同而不同 — 洋葱表皮细胞没有绿色叶绿体, 而叶片细胞里满是叶绿体。
🟫 Exam vs Reality🟫 考试 vs 真实
In the exam, draw the textbook cell with a nucleus. In real life, cells look very different depending on their job. Red blood cells have no nucleus at all. Muscle cells have many. The textbook picture is a general template, not a real specific cell.考试时画课本那张细胞图 (有核, 有各部分)。真实不同细胞长得完全 不一样: 红血球没核, 肌肉细胞有多个核。课本那张是拼出来的模板, 不是真实某个细胞。
