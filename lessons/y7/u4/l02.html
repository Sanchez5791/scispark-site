Using a Microscope使用显微镜

A microbiologist, a pathologist and a forensic scientist all use a microscope every day. A pathologist looks at tumour cells; a forensic scientist looks at hairs and fibres from a crime scene.微生物学家、病理学家、法医每天都用显微镜。病理学家看肿瘤细胞; 法医看犯罪现场的毛发和纤维。

Using a Microscope使用显微镜

•
A light microscope has an eyepiece (you look through it), objective lenses (low and high power), a stage (holds the slide), a focus knob, and a light source.光学显微镜有目镜(从这里看)、物镜(低倍和高倍)、载物台(放载 玻片)、调焦旋钮和光源。

•
Always start on LOW power to find the cell, then switch to HIGH power to see detail.总是先用低倍找到细胞,再换高倍看细节。
•
Total magnification = eyepiece x objective. Example: eyepiece 10 x objective 40 = 400. The image looks 400 times bigger than real life.总放大倍数 = 目镜 x 物镜。例:目镜 10 x 物镜 40 = 400。图像看起来 比真实大 400 倍。
•
Glass slides are fragile. Never force the high-power objective down onto a slide. Do not stare into the light source.玻璃载玻片易碎。绝不要把高倍物镜强行往下压向载玻片。不要盯着 光源看。
🟥 RED CARD · Common mistake🟥 红牌 · 常见错误
✘ The error✘ 错误想法
Many students think a higher magnification always shows more useful detail.很多同学以为放大倍数越高,看到的有用细节就越多。
Why it's wrong为什么错
At very high power the field of view is tiny and the image can be blurry if not focused well; you also see less of the sample.倍数很高时视野很小,没调好焦还会模糊;而且看到的样本范围更小。
✓ Correct idea✓ 正确想法
Use low power first to find the cell, then high power only for detail.
