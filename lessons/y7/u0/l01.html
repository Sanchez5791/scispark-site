Before any experiment, a scientist asks the right question

做任何实验之前,科学家先问对问题
🌍 Real-world · 真实情境
• A food scientist who develops new drinks does not start by mixing chemicals.
• They start with a clear, testable question such as "Does more sugar change how fast a drink goes flat?" The whole job depends on asking a question that an experiment can actually answer.
• 研发新饮料的食品科学家,不是一开始就乱混化学品。
• 他们先定一个清楚、可测试的问题,例如 「加更多糖会不会改变饮料变没气的速度?」 这份工作能不能做,全看问题问得对不对。
📘
What this lesson is about
这一课讲什么
• Science starts with a question.
• 科学从一个问题开始。
• But not every question can be answered by an experiment.
• 但不是每个问题都能用实验回答。
• This lesson: which questions are scientific, and how to turn a question into a hypothesis and a prediction.
• 这一课:哪些问题是科学问题,以及怎么把问题变成假设和预测。
⏰ Warm-up · 热身回顾
• Before you start: think of one experiment you've seen.
• What was being changed, and what was measured?
• 开始前:想一个你看过的实验。
• 它在改变什么?又在测量什么?
🔎 Plain · 白话
• A weak question makes the whole experiment wobble.
• A clear question gives the experiment a backbone.
• 问题问歪,整个实验就歪。
• 问题问清楚,实验才站得住。
🧩 Analogy · 比喻
A scientific question is like a game mission: it must tell you what to change and what score to measure.
科学问题像游戏任务:要说清你改什么,也要说清看哪个分数。
😄 Humour · 幽默
If the question is "Which drink has the best vibes?", the lab coat quietly walks away.
如果问题是「哪杯饮料最有感觉?」实验袍会自己逃走。

Asking Scientific Questions

学习重点
🔎
What makes a question scientific?
什么样的问题才是科学问题?
• A scientific question can be tested or measured with an experiment.
• 科学问题是可以用实验测试或测量的问题。
• It usually compares or measures something you can change.
• 它通常在比较或测量某个你可以改变的东西。
• Examples (each can be tested):
• 例子(每个都能测试):
• How does sugar concentration affect how fast a drink goes flat?
• 糖的浓度怎样影响饮料变没气的速度?
• How does the width of a parachute affect how fast it falls?
• 降落伞的宽度怎样影响它下落的速度?
• How does the distance of a lamp affect the rate of photosynthesis?
• 灯的距离怎样影响光合作用的速度?
🔎 Plain · 白话
Testable means you can actually do something, measure something, and compare the result.
可测试就是:真的能动手、能量数字、能比较结果。
🧠 Mnemonic · 口诀
Change one thing. Measure one result. That is the science-question lock.
口诀:改一件,量一个,科学问题才开锁。

⚖️
Scientific questions vs questions science cannot test
科学问题 vs 科学无法测试的问题
• Scientific (testable): "How does the type of game affect a phone's battery life?" — you can change the game and measure battery drain.
• 科学问题(可测试):「玩哪种游戏会怎样影响手机电池续航?」
• 你可以换游戏,然后测电量掉得多快。
• Not scientific (cannot be tested by experiment):
• 不是科学问题(实验无法测试):
• Questions of opinion ("Which game is the most fun?")
• 看法/喜好问题(「哪个游戏最好玩?」)
• Questions with no measurable outcome
• 没有可测量结果的问题
• Questions that cannot be fairly changed and measured
• 无法公平地改变和测量的问题
🧩 Analogy · 比喻
• Opinion questions are like asking favourite ice cream flavour.
• Science questions are like measuring melt time.
• 看法问题像问最爱哪种冰淇淋。
• 科学问题像量它多久融化。
😄 Humour · 幽默
• "Best game" starts an argument.
• "Battery drops by how much?" starts an experiment.
• 「哪个游戏最好玩」会吵架。
• 「电量掉多少」才会开实验。

🧪
A hypothesis is a possible explanation you can test
假设是一个可以测试的「可能解释」
• A hypothesis is a testable statement (a possible explanation), not a question.
• 假设是一句可测试的陈述(一个可能的解释),不是问题。
• It often links a cause and an effect.
• 它通常把「原因」和「结果」连起来。
• Example: "Increasing the sugar concentration makes the drink go flat faster."
• 例子:「提高糖的浓度会让饮料更快变没气。
• 」
🔎 Plain · 白话
• A hypothesis is not the question.
• It is your best possible answer before testing.
• 假设不是问题。
• 它是你实验前给出的可能答案。
🧩 Analogy · 比喻
Think detective story: the question is the case; the hypothesis is the suspect.
像侦探故事:问题是案子,假设是嫌疑人。
🧩
A prediction says what will happen if the hypothesis is true
预测说的是「如果假设成立,会发生什么」
• A prediction is a specific statement of what you expect to observe.
• 预测是一句具体说明「你预计会观察到什么」的话。
• It usually has an "if ... then ..." shape.
• 它通常是「如果......那么......」的形状。
• Example: "If the sugar concentration is higher, then the drink will lose its fizz in less time."
• 例子:「如果糖的浓度更高,那么饮料会在更短时间内失去气泡。
• 」
🔎 Plain · 白话
A prediction says the visible result you expect to see if the hypothesis is right.
预测是说:如果假设对,你应该看到什么结果。
🧠 Mnemonic · 口诀
Hypothesis = why. Prediction = what you should see.
记法:假设讲为什么,预测讲会看到什么。
Hypothesis vs Prediction — they are NOT the same
假设 vs 预测 —— 两个不是同一个东西
🧩 Analogy · 比喻
• Hypothesis is your guess about the hidden cause.
• Prediction is the receipt you can check at the end.
• 假设像猜背后的原因。
• 预测像最后能对账的收据。
🧠 Mnemonic · 口诀
Question asks. Hypothesis explains. Prediction checks.
口诀:问题负责问,假设负责解释,预测负责检查。
🚩 COMMON MISTAKE · 常见错误
✘ Students often think · 学生常以为
Students treat "hypothesis" and "prediction" as the same thing, or write a question and call it a hypothesis.
学生把「假设」和「预测」当成同一个东西, 或者写了一个问题却把它叫成假设。
✔ Correct · 正确
• A hypothesis is a possible EXPLANATION (a statement, not a question).
• A prediction says what WILL be observed IF the hypothesis is true (often "if...then...").
• A question is neither — it is what you ask before forming either.
• 假设是一个可能的「解释」(是陈述句,不是问题)。
• 预测是说「如果假设成立,会观察到什么」(通常「如果......那么......」)。
• 问题两者都不是
• 问题是你在做出假设或预测之前先问的。
⚖️ Exam vs Reality · 考试 vs 真实
📝 In exams · 考试里
Exams often accept a simple "if...then..." line as both hypothesis and prediction without distinguishing them.
考试常常接受一句简单的「如果……那么……」, 既当假设又当预测,不去区分。
🔬 Real science · 真实科学
In real science a hypothesis (proposed explanation) and a prediction (expected observation) are distinct steps; the prediction is what is actually tested.
在真实科学里,假设(提出的解释)和预测(预期的观察) 是两个不同步骤;真正被测试的是预测。
🎯 What the examiner wants · 考官要什么
State = short answer · Describe = say what & how · Explain = give the reason (fact + because) · Define = use the exact term.
State=短答 · Describe=说什么、怎样 · Explain=讲原因(事实+because) · Define=用准确术语。

Q03 Rewrite · 2 marks · short_answer
The question "Which fizzy drink is the nicest?" is not
scientific. Rewrite it as a scientific question that could be tested.
问题「哪种汽水最好喝?」不是科学问题。
把它改写成一个可以测试的科学问题。
💬 Tip · 提示: Turn an opinion question into something testable and measurable. 把一个看法问题改成可测试、可测量的问题。
🔑 Hint · 提示
Replace "nicest" with something you can measure with a number.
把「最好喝」换成一个你能用数字测量的东西。
✅ Show answer · 看答案
Answer · 答案
A testable question changing one measurable variable, e.g. "How does the sugar concentration of a fizzy drink affect how fast it goes flat?"
一个改变某个可测量变量的可测试问题,例如 「汽水的糖浓度怎样影响它变没气的速度?」
Mark scheme · 评分: Accept a testable question that changes one measurable factor and measures one result, e.g. sugar concentration affecting time to go flat. Also accept different measurable drink outcomes such as fizz lost, pH, temperature change, or time taken, if only one factor is changed.
接受一个可测试问题:改变一个可测量因素,并测量一个结果。例如糖浓度影响变没气时间。若只改变一个因素,也接受其他可测量饮料结果,如气泡减少、pH、温度变化或所需时间。
If wrong · 答错: The word “nicest” is the trap. It is personal taste, not a measurement. Change it into something countable, such as time to go flat, sugar concentration, or fizz lost. 陷阱是「最好喝」。这是个人喜好,不是测量。要改成能量的东西,例如变没气时间、糖浓度、气泡减少。
Q04 Write · 2 marks · short_answer
A student asks: "How does the width of a parachute affect
how fast it falls?" Write a hypothesis for this investigation.
一位学生问:「降落伞的宽度怎样影响它下落的速度?」
为这个探究写一个假设。
💬 Tip · 提示: A hypothesis is a statement linking a cause to an effect. 假设是一句把原因连到结果的陈述。
🔑 Hint · 提示
Do not write a question. Write what you think the answer is.
不要写问题。写出你认为答案是什么。
✅ Show answer · 看答案
Answer · 答案
A statement linking width to fall speed, e.g. "A wider parachute falls more slowly."
一句把宽度连到下落速度的陈述,例如 「更宽的降落伞下落得更慢。」
Mark scheme · 评分: any clear statement linking width and fall speed (either direction stated); accept: A statement linking width to fall speed, e.g. "A wider parachute falls more slowly."; accept also: a clear statement linking cause and effect.
任何清楚把宽度和下落速度连起来的陈述(方向写明即可)；接受：一句把宽度连到下落速度的陈述,例如 「更宽的降落伞下落得更慢。」；也接受：清楚把原因和结果连起来的陈述句。
If wrong · 答错: You may have written the question again. A hypothesis must be a statement that links parachute width to falling speed, with a clear direction. 你可能只是把问题重写了。假设必须是陈述句,把降落伞宽度和下落速度连起来,而且方向要清楚。
Q05 Write · 3 marks · short_answer
For the hypothesis "A wider parachute falls more slowly,"
write a prediction in an "if ... then ..." form.
对于假设「更宽的降落伞下落得更慢」,
用「如果……那么……」的形式写一个预测。
💬 Tip · 提示: A prediction has an "if...then..." shape and says what you will observe. 预测是「如果……那么……」的形状,说出你会观察到什么。
🔑 Hint · 提示
Start with "If the parachute is wider, then..."
用「如果降落伞更宽,那么……」开头。
🔑 Hint 2 · 第二提示
The "then" part must be something you could time or measure.
「那么」那部分必须是你能计时或测量的东西。
✅ Show answer · 看答案
Answer · 答案
"If a parachute is wider, then it will take more time to reach the ground."
「如果降落伞更宽,那么它会用更多时间到达地面。」
Mark scheme · 评分: Accept an “if...then...” prediction matching the hypothesis direction: if parachute width increases, then fall time increases / fall speed decreases. The “then” part must be observable or measurable.
接受与假设方向一致的「如果……那么……」预测:如果降落伞宽度增加,那么下落时间增加/下落速度降低。「那么」部分必须可观察或可测量。
If wrong · 答错: A prediction is the testable “then” result. Do not only repeat “wider parachutes fall slowly”; say what you would measure, such as longer time to reach the ground. 预测要写可测试的「那么」结果。不要只重复「更宽会更慢」,要写会量到什么,例如到地时间更长。

Answer · 答案
(a) "More salt makes ice melt faster." (b) "If there is more salt, then the ice will melt in less time."
(a)「更多盐让冰融化得更快。」 (b)「如果有更多盐,那么冰会在更短时间内融化。」
Mark scheme · 评分: Accept both parts: (a) a statement linking salt amount to melting speed; (b) a matching “if...then...” prediction with a measurable result, such as more salt causing shorter melting time.
两部分都接受才完整：(a) 陈述盐量和融化速度的关系；(b) 对应的「如果……那么……」预测,并有可测量结果,例如盐更多使融化时间更短。
If wrong · 答错: There are two jobs here. The hypothesis links salt to melting speed; the prediction turns that into an “if...then...” measurable result. If both lines look the same, one part is missing. 这里有两件事。假设把盐和融化速度连起来；预测把它改成「如果……那么……」的可测结果。如果两句长一样,就少了一部分。

Key points to keep
记住这几点
• Scientific question: can be tested or measured by experiment.
• 科学问题:能用实验测试或测量。
• Hypothesis: a possible explanation (a statement, not a question).
• 假设:一个可能的解释(陈述句,不是问题)。
• Prediction: what you expect to observe, often "if...then...".
• 预测:你预计会观察到什么,通常「如果......那么......」。
• Hypothesis and prediction are different steps.
• 假设和预测是不同的步骤。

🔎 Plain · 白话
• Keep this chain: Question → Hypothesis → Prediction → Test.
• Break the chain, and the experiment loses direction.
• 记住这条链:问题→假设→预测→测试。
• 链断了,实验就没方向。
