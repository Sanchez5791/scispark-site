01 · Hook

A spoonful of sugar in your tea — where does it go?

It seems to vanish. But the tea is sweeter. So the sugar must still be there — just invisibly small. Today we look at matter through that lens.

Why this matters

Particles are too small to see, yet they explain almost every change you can taste, smell, and feel today.

02 · Learn

Matter is made of particles

Watch Professor Spark explain it at the blackboard, then read the summary.

Video placeholder · style A · Khan blackboard
Transcript

Imagine you keep cutting a sugar grain in half, again and again. Eventually you reach a piece so tiny you cannot cut it further without changing what it is. That tiny piece is a particle.

In summary

All matter is made of particles. Particles are far too small to see with the eye. The way particles move and arrange themselves decides whether the matter behaves as a solid, a liquid, or a gas.
