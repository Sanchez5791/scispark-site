SCREEN 1 · HOOK
Rutherford's Gold Foil Experiment
Rutherford 的金箔实验
⏰ 热身 · Warm-up hook
A few particles bounced back like they hit a hidden wall inside the foil.
少数粒子像撞到金箔里的隐藏墙一样反弹。
  • Rutherford fired tiny particles at gold foil — and saw something that made him doubt everything: a few bounced straight back.
  • Rutherford 用小粒子打金箔 — 看到一件让他怀疑一切的事: 少数粒子竟然直接反弹回来.
  • Who does this today?
  • A nuclear physicist — they fire particles in huge accelerators to study what atoms are made of, continuing Rutherford's work.
  • 今天谁在做这个?
  • 核物理学家 — 他们在巨型加速器里发射粒子, 研究原子由什么组成, 延续 Rutherford 的工作.
  • At the time, everyone believed Thomson's plum pudding model — a soft ball of charge.
  • If that were true, all the tiny particles should pass straight through.
  • The experiment tested that idea.
  • 当时人人相信 Thomson 的布丁模型 — 一团软软的电荷.
  • 如果是真的, 所有小粒子都应该直穿过去.
  • 这个实验就是要检验这个想法.
SCREEN 2 · LEARN
Learn — Rutherford's Gold Foil Experiment
机制 · Rutherford 的金箔实验
  • Rutherford shot tiny particles (alpha particles) at a very thin sheet of gold and watched where they went.
  • Rutherford 把很小的粒子 (alpha 粒子) 射向一张很薄的金箔, 看它们去了哪里.
  • Most alpha particles passed straight through the foil.
  • A small number changed direction, and a very small number came straight back toward the source.
  • 多数 alpha 粒子直接穿过金箔.
  • 少数改变了方向, 极少数直接朝源头弹回.
  • OBSERVATION (what was seen): most particles passed straight through; a few were deflected; very few bounced straight back.
  • INFERENCE (what it means): the atom is mostly empty space, with a tiny, dense, positive nucleus at the centre.
  • 观察 (observation, 看到了什么): 多数粒子直穿; 少数偏转; 极少数直接弹回.
  • 推论 (inference, 这说明什么): 原子大部分是空的, 中心有一个很小、很密、 带正电的原子核 (nucleus).
✶ 考试 vs 真实 · Exam vs Reality
  • In the exam, draw the atom with a small nucleus in the middle.
  • In real life, atoms are almost completely empty — if an atom were a football field, the nucleus would be a grain of rice.
  • 考试时画: 原子中间有一个核.
  • 真实情况: 原子 99% 是空的.
  • 如果原子是足球场, 原子核只是一粒米.
  • A hypothesis is a prediction you test.
  • Thomson's model predicted ALL particles would pass straight through.
  • 假设 (hypothesis) 是一个可检验的预测.
  • Thomson 的模型预测所有粒子都会直穿.
  • The observation did NOT match the prediction — a few particles bounced back.
  • When observation does not match the hypothesis, scientists revise the model.
  • That is how Rutherford replaced the plum pudding model with the nuclear model.
  • 观察跟预测不一致 — 少数粒子弹回了.
  • 当观察跟假设不符, 科学家就修正模型.
  • Rutherford 就是这样用核模型取代了布丁模型.
❌ 常见错误 · Common mistake — don't fall for this!
  • Common mistake — don't fall for this!
  • 常见错误 — 别上当!
  • Students think the atom (and its nucleus) is a solid ball with no empty space.
  • 学生以为原子 (和它的核) 是实心的, 没有空隙.
  • ❌ Many students think an atom is a solid ball, packed full of matter with no gaps.
  • ❌ 很多同学以为原子是一颗实心球, 里面塞满物质, 没有空隙.
  • Think of throwing balls at a wide net.
  • Most go straight through the open part; only a few hit a string and bounce.
  • In the gold foil experiment most alpha particles went straight through — that only works if the atom is mostly empty.
  • If an atom were a football field, the nucleus would be a single grain of rice in the centre.
  • 想象把球扔向一张大网.
  • 多数从空的地方直穿, 只有少数撞到网线弹回.
  • 金箔实验里多数 alpha 粒子直穿 — 只有原子大部分是空的才会这样.
  • 如果原子是一个足球场, 原子核只是中心的一粒米.
  • ✓ Correct idea: the atom is about 99% empty space.
  • The nucleus is tiny and dense and sits at the centre; the rest is empty.
  • ✓ 正确想法: 原子大约 99% 是空的.
  • 原子核又小又密, 在中心; 其余都是空的.
🧠 口诀金句 · Golden phrase
Most through mostly empty. Few bounce tiny dense nucleus.
多数穿过＝大多是空。少数反弹＝小而密的核。
