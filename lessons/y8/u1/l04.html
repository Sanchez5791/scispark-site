质子、中子、电子
⏰ 热身 · Warm-up hook
Protons and neutrons are the heavy siblings. Electrons are the tiny ones.
质子和中子是重的兄弟；电子是很轻的小朋友。
Three particles, one atom — who carries the charge?一个原子，三种粒子 —— 谁带电？
  • Every atom is built from just three particles: protons, neutrons and electrons.
  • Some carry a charge.
  • Some do not.
  • 每个原子都由三种粒子组成：质子、中子、电子。
  • 有的带电，有的不带。

SCREEN 2 · LEARN
Learn — Protons, Neutrons, Electrons
机制 · 质子、中子、电子
The three subatomic particles三种亚原子粒子
  • An atom has a nucleus in the centre containing protons and neutrons.
  • Electrons are found in the region around the nucleus.
  • 原子中心有一个原子核，核里有质子和中子。
  • 电子在核外的区域里。

❌ 常见错误 · Common mistake — don't fall for this!
Common mistake — don't fall for this!常见错误 — 别上当！
  • Students think protons, neutrons and electrons are all the same size, and that an atom's mass proton + neutron + electron added together.
  • 学生以为质子、中子、电子都一样大， 又以为原子质量 质子 + 中子 + 电子全部相加。
  • Many students think all three particles are the same size.
  • NO.
  • 很多同学以为三种粒子一样大。
  • 错。
  • ✓ Proton and neutron have relative mass 1.
  • ✓ Electron is about 1/1840 — almost nothing.
  • So atomic mass ≈ protons + neutrons; the electrons are too light to count.
  • ✓ 质子和中子相对质量是 1。
  • ✓ 电子约 1/1840，几乎为零。
  • 所以原子质量 ≈ 质子数 + 中子数；电子太轻，忽略不计。

✶ 考试 vs 真实 · Exam vs Reality
  • In the exam, draw the atom with a small nucleus in the middle.
  • In real life, atoms are almost completely empty — if an atom were a football field, the nucleus would be a grain of rice.
  • 考试时画：原子中间有一个核。
  • 真实情况：原子 99% 是空的。
  • 如果原子是足球场，原子核只是一粒米。
  • In the exam, draw the atom with a small nucleus in the middle.
  • 考试时画：原子中间有一个小原子核。
  • In real life, atoms are almost completely empty; a football-field atom would have a rice-grain nucleus.
  • 真实情况：原子几乎全是空的；如果原子像足球场，核只有一粒米。

What you now know你现在掌握了
  • Proton +1, neutron 0, electron -1.
  • Proton and neutron have relative mass 1; the electron is ~1/1840.
  • Atomic number number of protons.
  • Atomic mass ≈ protons + neutrons.
  • 质子 +1，中子 0，电子 -1。
  • 质子和中子相对质量 1，电子约 1/1840。
  • 原子序数 质子数。
  • 原子质量 ≈ 质子 + 中子。
