Why Don't Electrons Just Fly Away?
电子为什么不飞走?
⏰ 热身 · Warm-up hook
Opposite charges are like magnets with a crush: they pull together fast.
异种电荷像互相喜欢的磁铁：一靠近就拉在一起。
✶ 考试 vs 真实 · Exam vs Reality
  • Rub a plastic comb on your hair, then hold it over small bits of paper.
  • The paper jumps up to the comb.
  • Something invisible pulls them together.
  • The same kind of invisible pull holds the tiny electrons close to the centre of every atom.
  • 拿塑料梳子在头发上摩擦, 再靠近小纸屑, 纸屑会跳起来黏住梳子。
  • 有一种看不见的力把它们拉在一起。
  • 同样一种看不见的拉力, 把小小的电子 拉在每个原子的中心附近。
  • Try it with any comb or balloon you have.
  • The rubbed object picks up paper bits.
  • 用任何梳子或气球试试。
  • 摩擦过的东西能吸起纸屑。
  • WARNING: For the static-rub experiment (PVC + paper bits), keep it away from electronic devices.
  • 警告: 静电摩擦实验 (PVC + 纸屑) 要避免靠近电子设备。

SCREEN 2 · LEARN
Learn — Why Don't Electrons Just Fly Away?
机制 · 电子为什么不飞走?
Opposite charges pull together异电相吸
  • An atom has a centre called the nucleus (核).
  • The nucleus has a positive charge (+) because of its protons.
  • The electrons around it have a negative charge (-).
  • A positive charge and a negative charge pull toward each other.
  • This pull is called electrostatic attraction (静电吸引).
  • It is what keeps the electrons near the nucleus.
  • 原子有一个中心, 叫 nucleus (核)。
  • 核因为有质子而带正电 (+)。
  • 核外的电子带负电 (-)。
  • 正电和负电会互相拉。
  • 这种拉力叫 electrostatic attraction (静电吸引)。
  • 就是它把电子拉在核的附近。

✶ 考试 vs 真实 · Exam vs Reality
✶ 考试 vs 真实 · Exam vs Reality
  • In the exam, draw electrons going around the nucleus in circles.
  • In real life, electrons don't follow fixed paths — they form a 'cloud' where we only know the chance of finding them.
  • 考试 时 画: 电子 绕 原子核 转 圆 圈。
  • 真实 情况: 电子 没 固定 路径, 它 像 云 一样 散 在 原子 周围。
Common mistake — don't fall for this!常见错误 — 别上当!
  • Many students think electrons run on fixed circular rails around the nucleus, like planets on tracks.
  • 很多同学以为电子在固定圆轨道上绕核跑, 像行星在轨道上。
  • [IMAGE shows: NOT rails — a fuzzy cloud held in by an inward pull.] The electrons are held near the nucleus by the attraction between (+) and (-), not by any rail or track.
  • [图: 不是轨道 — 是被向内的拉力固定住的电子云。
  • ] 电子是被正电和负电之间的吸引拉在核附近, 不是靠什么轨道。
  • ✓ Correct: electrons sit in a cloud, held in place by electrostatic attraction (pull), not by fixed rails.
  • ✓ 正确: 电子在电子云里, 靠静电吸引 (拉力) 固定, 不是靠 固定轨道。
  • Students think electrons orbit the nucleus on fixed circular rails like planets.
  • 学生以为电子像行星一样在固定圆轨道绕核。

总结 · 串联
What holds the atom together是什么把原子拉在一起
  • The nucleus is positive (+).
  • The electrons are negative (-).
  • Opposite charges attract — this electrostatic attraction holds the electrons near the nucleus.
  • More protons means a stronger pull.
  • 核带正电 (+)。
  • 电子带负电 (-)。
  • 异电相吸 — 这种静电吸引把 电子拉在核附近。
  • 质子越多, 拉力越强。
  • Going deeper: atoms can lose or gain electrons to become ions — you'll learn that in Y9 Unit 1.
  • The electrons also sit in layers (shells) — Y9 Unit 2 teaches electron shells.
  • 更深的内容: 原子可以失去或得到电子变成 ion (离子) — Y9 Unit 1 会教。
  • 电子也排在 layers (shell, 层) 里 — Y9 Unit 2 教 electron shell。
  • We've finished looking inside the atom.
  • Next we step out to real substances — and ask: is the water you drink actually pure?
  • 我们看完了原子内部。
  • 接下来走出去看现实物质 — 问一句: 你喝的水真的纯吗?
