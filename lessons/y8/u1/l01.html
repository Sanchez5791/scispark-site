SCREEN 1 · HOOK
How Did We Discover the Atom?
我们是怎么发现原子的?
⏰ 热身 · Warm-up hook
Old atom models are like old phone maps: not stupid, just missing the newest road.
旧原子模型像旧手机地图：不是笨，只是还没更新。
  • You think an atom is a solid little ball?
  • 100 years ago scientists thought so too — until experiments rewrote everything.
  • 你以为原子是一颗实心球?
  • 100 年前科学家也这样想 — 直到一连串实验改写了一切.
  • Everything around you — water, a coin, the air — is made of atoms.
  • But no one has ever seen one directly.
  • 你身边的一切 — 水, 硬币, 空气 — 都由原子组成.
  • 但没人直接看过一颗原子.
  • Scientists could not see atoms, so they built MODELS from EVIDENCE.
  • The model changed three times in about 100 years.
  • 科学家看不到原子, 所以他们用证据 (evidence) 建立模型 (model).
  • 这个模型在大约 100 年里改了三次.
SCREEN 2 · LEARN
Learn — How Did We Discover the Atom?
机制 · 我们是怎么发现原子的?
  • A model is a scientist's best picture of something too small to see.
  • 模型就是科学家对看不见的小东西画出的最好的图.
  • Like drawing a map of a place you cannot enter — you update the map when you get new information.
  • 就像给一个进不去的地方画地图 — 拿到新消息就更新地图.
  • Dalton (1803): atom is a tiny solid ball that cannot be split.
  • Thomson (1897): "plum pudding" — a ball of positive charge with electrons dotted inside.
  • Rutherford (1911): a tiny dense nucleus in the centre with electrons outside.
  • Dalton (1803): 原子是一颗不能再分的实心小球.
  • Thomson (1897): 葡萄干布丁模型 — 一团正电里散布着电子.
  • Rutherford (1911): 中心有一个很小很密的原子核, 电子在外面.
✶ 考试 vs 真实 · Exam vs Reality
  • In the exam, draw solid particles touching in a regular pattern.
  • In real life there are tiny gaps between them, held by forces.
  • 考试时画: 粒子紧挨着, 排列规则.
  • 真实情况: 粒子之间其实有很小的缝隙, 靠吸引力拉在一起.
  • The model changed because scientists found NEW EVIDENCE.
  • 模型会改, 是因为科学家找到了新证据 (new evidence).
  • Each new experiment gave evidence the old model could not explain, so scientists built a better model.
  • Science changes with evidence, not with opinion.
  • 每个新实验都给出旧模型解释不了的证据, 所以科学家建立更好的模型.
  • 科学靠证据改变, 不是靠个人意见.
❌ 常见错误 · Common mistake — don't fall for this!
  • Common mistake — don't fall for this!
  • 常见错误 — 别上当!
  • Students think the early atomic models (Dalton, Thomson) were simply wrong and useless.
  • 学生以为早期的原子模型 (Dalton、Thomson) 只是错的、 没有用.
  • ❌ Many students think the old models were just wrong and a waste of time.
  • ❌ 很多同学以为旧模型只是错的, 浪费时间.
  • Compare the models: Dalton's solid ball fitted all the evidence known in 1803.
  • Thomson's plum pudding fitted the new evidence about electrons in 1897.
  • Each model was the best fit for the evidence of its time — not a silly mistake.
  • 对比一下: Dalton 的实心球符合 1803 年所有已知证据.
  • Thomson 的布丁模型符合 1897 年关于电子的新证据.
  • 每个模型都是当时证据下最好的图 — 不是愚蠢的错误.
  • ✓ Correct idea: each model was the BEST FIT for the evidence of its time, then IMPROVED when new evidence appeared.
  • Models are upgraded, not thrown away as "useless".
  • ✓ 正确想法: 每个模型都是当时证据下最合适的, 等新证据出现就被改进.
  • 模型是被升级, 不是当成"没用"丢掉.
🧠 口诀金句 · Golden phrase
D-T-R: Dalton ball, Thomson pudding, Rutherford nucleus.
D-T-R：Dalton 小球，Thomson 布丁，Rutherford 找到核。
